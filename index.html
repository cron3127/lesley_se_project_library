Readers Picks

That our readers love

  • Life Admin: How I Learned to Do Less, Do Better, and Live More

    Readers will consider this book to be between the sphere of self help and nonfiction. After reading this book, many like myself will likely start to use the term life admin because they will realize that some of their tasks can be catergorized as that. Readers will love that this book spotlights invisible labor that is performed by all in some capacity especially women. In all, this book is perfect for readers who want to find out the hidden reasons why they are tired of or those that simply want to make their daily lives easier in some way.

    Author: Elizabeth F. Emens

  • Killers of the Flower Moon: The Osage Murders and the Birth of the FBI

    This book is perfect for nonfiction lovers out there in the wild. The book felt like you were watching a great documentary film or a riveting film based on a true story. The pace of Killers of the Flower Moon was fast, but keeps readers engaged. Most likely because the writing is concise, hence easy to follow along and understand. This book is highly recommended for nonfiction lovers or those wanting to get into the nonfiction genre.

    Author: David Grann

  • Kendra Donovan Mystery Series

    Readers will fall in love with this series. It is mix of time travel, romance, and mystery. In addition, the series setting has an equal focus on London high and low society along with the technological/societal caused by the industrial revolution. It truly makes readers feel like they are in 1800s England. Books 1-6 are now available at libraries near you.

    Author: Julie McElwain

  • You Deserve Each Other

    This book is for all the romance lovers out there. It has enemies to lovers, second chance romance, and crazy but realistic love story. Equally as enriching, is the book's side characters along with main couple's growth within their relationship and themselves.

    Author: Sarah Hogle

About the library

Libraries are essential establishments in practically every city, and the Triple Peaks Library is no exception. It first opened its doors way back in 2018, right as our humble town began bidding welcome to its very first citizens!

Since its initial opening, the library has seen nearly 342 monthly visitors. That's almost 50% of our population! So far, most citizens have enjoyed reading books and using our ample online resources.

Become a member today, and make sure to check out our Staff Picks section. This week's selection is full of books anyone interested in computer programming would love!
